Learn about the

Ford Model T

ENGINE

The Ford Model T, invented by Henry Ford, was released on October 1, 1908. It has an enclosed four-cylinder engine with a detachable cylinder head.

HORN

Miller Reese Hutchinson patened the Klaxon (“ahooga”) horn in 1908. The Klaxon horn was used in most Model T’s and is known for it’s unforgettable sound.

HEADLIGHTS

The headlights in a Ford Model T were Acetylene lamps fueled by Acetylene gas. To start the light the driver is required to open the headlight cover and light the gas to produce a flame that the headlight lense reflects.

STEERING WHEEL

The Model T had a left-sided steering wheel and the transmission was controlled by three foot pedals and a hand lever.

WHEELS

Before 1925 all Ford wheels had rims that clinched the wheel into place. Fun Fact: Ford factories produced more than 15 million Model Ts between 1913 and 1927.
